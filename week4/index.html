Red Alert 2 Low-Poly Soviet Base Documentation

Project Overview

This project recreates three iconic Soviet units and structures from Command & Conquer: Red Alert 2 — the Rhino Tank, Sentry Gun, and Power Plant — in a low-poly style.

Ideation and References

The main idea was to reinterpret the Soviet base aesthetics from Red Alert 2 in a minimal yet recognizable form. The Rhino Tank reference was taken from a three-view image found online, while the Sentry Gun reference was generated using AI. However, since the AI-generated image was not completely accurate, I adjusted proportions manually to match the Red Alert 2 visual style.

Modeling Process

All three models were built in Blender using a low-poly workflow emphasizing modifier efficiency and symmetry. The Mirror Modifier was applied throughout to ensure perfect bilateral alignment, while the Solidify Modifier was used on the Rhino Tank’s cannon barrel to give it realistic thickness.

Reference Setup

I imported the three-view Rhino Tank reference image into Blender and aligned it in front, side, and top orthographic views. For the Power Plant and Sentry Gun, I modeled primarily from perspective references, focusing on silhouette clarity rather than 1:1 accuracy.

Setting up the Rhino Tank reference image inside Blender for accurate proportion matching.

Using the Sentry Gun AI reference image as a modeling base; adjustments made for symmetry and scale.

Modeling Techniques

The modeling process involved blocking out basic shapes with cubes and cylinders, then refining details using edge loops and extrusions. I focused on maintaining a clean topology to ensure the models remained low-poly while still capturing essential details.

Final Renders

The final renders show the three low-poly models together, lit with an HDRI environment and a directional key light to emphasize material contrast and geometric depth.

Reflection

This project was a rewarding exercise in low-poly modeling and stylized design. It challenged me to distill complex designs into their most essential forms while retaining recognizability. I learned a lot about using Blender's modifiers effectively and the importance of reference images in achieving accurate proportions. Overall, I am pleased with how the models turned out and look forward to applying these techniques in future projects.
