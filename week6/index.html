Autonomous Vehicle Interface Documentation

Concept Overview

This project explores how the user interface of a car transforms once the vehicle becomes fully autonomous. The interaction shifts from manual driving to system engagement — the vehicle evolves into a smart electronic device rather than a mechanical one. Inspired by BMW’s interior design philosophy, the concept emphasizes digital minimalism and material contrast, merging advanced technology with tactile craftsmanship.

Reference — BMW’s contemporary interior design language, balancing digital and physical interfaces.

Initial hand-drawn sketch visualizing the autonomous console as an interactive digital surface.

Modeling Process and Design Choices

The design was built in Blender, starting with the dashboard as the central form. The Subdivision Surface and Mirror Modifiers were applied to achieve smooth, symmetrical geometry. The trim uses a wood material with aluminum wrapping along the edges to create a tactile, precise finish. A separate climate control screen was introduced for dual-zone operation between the driver and co-driver. The curved main display was modeled by cutting through a cylindrical mesh to generate a continuous, immersive viewing surface. Lastly, the cup holder was carved using a Boolean subtraction with a cylinder, ensuring both functionality and design coherence.

Reflection and Next Steps

This project represents an early stage exploration of future in-car interaction design. The next step focuses on redefining the co-driver’s role — developing shared interaction zones, adaptive ambient lighting, and contextual passenger information systems that extend the same design logic beyond the driver’s seat.
